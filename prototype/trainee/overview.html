Traineeships

A traineeship is a course designed to prepare someone for an apprenticeship or get them ready for work. They can take up to 6 months.

A traineeship includes:

  • help with English and maths (if needed)
  • a work experience placement (lasting at least 6 weeks)

Traineeships are unpaid, although employers are encouraged to support trainees with expenses such as travel and meals.

How to apply

Anyone can apply for a traineeship if they’re:

  • eligible to work in England
  • unemployed and have little or no work experience
  • aged 16-18 and qualified below Level 3 or aged 19-23 and qualified below Level 2

Get advice

If you’re thinking of applying for a trainseeship, or are unsure whether it’s the right option for you, contact the National Careers Service website for advice.
